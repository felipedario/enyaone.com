New Age, Celtic, ambient and ethereal music: artists like Enya

New Age music, often said to be Enya's musical genre, is a style originally associated with some New Age beliefs (but it's often not the case; Enya herself is catholic). It has its basis in the 1960s and is generally characterised as being primarily instrumental and repetitively melodic in nature.

New age

New Age music is a genre that's all about creating a peaceful and relaxing atmosphere. Think calming sounds, soothing melodies, and an overall feeling of tranquility.

Characteristics

  • Atmosphere is key: The main goal is to evoke a sense of serenity, often used for relaxation, meditation, yoga, or even just creating a calm environment at home.
  • Instrumentation: It can be quite diverse! You'll find acoustic instruments like piano, harp, and flute, alongside electronic instruments and nature sounds (think flowing water or birdsong).
  • Melody and Harmony: Generally features gentle melodies and harmonies, avoiding complex structures or jarring sounds. Tempo: Usually slow and flowing, helping to create that relaxed vibe.

Purpose

While it can simply be enjoyed for its beauty, New Age music often has a functional purpose:

  • Stress reduction: Its calming nature can help lower anxiety and promote relaxation.
  • Mindfulness and meditation: It can help you focus your thoughts and achieve a meditative state.
  • Inspiration and creativity: Some find it sparks their imagination and helps them connect with their inner selves.

Key Artists

Some of the most well-known New Age artists include:

  • Enya: Known for her ethereal vocals and Celtic influences.
  • Yanni: A pianist and composer known for his large-scale orchestral performances.
  • George Winston: A pianist known for his minimalist and melodic compositions.
  • Kitaro: A Japanese composer who blends electronic and acoustic instruments.

Celtic music

Celtic music today is a vibrant and evolving genre that honors its deep roots while embracing modern influences. It's more than just traditional jigs and reels - it's a dynamic blend of old and new, acoustic and electronic, local and global.

Fusion and Experimentation

Blending genres: Celtic music is increasingly fusing with other genres like rock, pop, electronic, world music, and even classical. This leads to exciting new sounds and collaborations.

Contemporary Interpretations:

  • Modernizing the sound: Artists like Enya and Loreena McKennitt have achieved global success by incorporating Celtic elements into their contemporary styles, often with New Age or ethereal influences.
  • Expanding the audience: This approach introduces Celtic music to a broader audience who might not be familiar with the traditional forms.

  • Clannad is a highly influential Irish musical group.   • The Chieftains: Legends of Irish traditional music, known for their virtuosity and collaborations with diverse artists.
  • The Dubliners: A hugely influential Irish folk group with a long history of lively pub songs and ballads.
  • Afro Celt Sound System: A groundbreaking group that fuses Irish music with West African rhythms and electronica.
  • Loreena McKennitt: A Canadian singer-songwriter who blends Celtic influences with world music elements.
  • Celtic Woman: An all-female Irish musical ensemble known for their beautiful harmonies, traditional and contemporary songs, and theatrical stage shows. They perform a blend of Celtic music, classical crossover, and adult contemporary.

Ethereal and ambiente music

Both ethereal and ambient music aim to create immersive sonic experiences, but they have distinct characteristics:

Ethereal Music

  • Dreamy and otherworldly: Imagine floating through clouds or exploring a fantastical realm. Ethereal music often evokes a sense of wonder, mystery, and transcendence.
  • Focus on atmosphere and texture: Melodies may be present but are often secondary to the overall mood and sonic landscape.
  • Common elements: Reverb, delay, and other effects are used to create a spacious, atmospheric sound. Vocals are often layered and processed to sound angelic or otherworldly.
  • Examples: Enya, Cocteau Twins, Dead Can Dance, Sigur Ros

Ambient Music:

  • Calm and atmospheric: Think of a peaceful environment, like a quiet forest or a starry night sky. Ambient music is designed to create a calming and immersive atmosphere.
  • Minimalism and subtlety: It often features slow tempos, long drones, and subtle shifts in sound. Melodies and rhythms are often understated or absent altogether.
  • "Background" listening: While it can be enjoyed attentively, ambient music is often used to create a mood or enhance an environment without demanding focused attention.
  • Examples: Brian Eno, Aphex Twin, Stars of the Lid, Tim Hecker
