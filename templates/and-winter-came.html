And Winter Came... (2008)

Enya's album titled And Winter Came...emerged on the music scene in 2008, with its UK release on November 10th and US release on November 11th. The collection of songs primarily embraces a Christmas or Winter theme, immersing listeners in a seasonal ambiance. The album made its mark on the UK charts, peaking at #6 on November 16, 2008, reflecting its popularity among listeners. Since its initial release, And Winter Came... has garnered significant commercial success, surpassing 2,100,000 copies sold. This achievement further solidifies Enya's enduring presence in the music industry.

Stream or download Enya songs

Enya's album comprises a collection of twelve tracks, including ten original English compositions. Among them, "Oiche Chiuin" stands out, sung in Gaelic, and a rendition of "Oh Come, Oh Come Emmanuel" performed in Latin. Notably, "Oiche Chiuin" presents a unique occurrence where Enya re-recorded a previously released track, originally unveiled in the early 1990s.

Diverging from the pattern of previous Enya albums, which often saw the release of CD singles accompanied by bonus tracks, it seems that there are no plans for physical media singles from this album. Instead, the focus lies on digital downloads.

Enya, expressing her thoughts on her website, shared her long-standing desire to create a Christmas album. However, during the recording process, she "didn't feel it was right to impose a Christmas theme on certain songs", leading to a more nuanced approach in crafting this collection.

Nicky Ryan, her producer since the first album, adds that they "started out writing for a Christmas album, but it has evolved into more an Enya album based in a winter landscape where Christmas arrives here and there, but it would be wrong to call it an Enya Christmas album."

Roma Ryan, Enya's lyricist, also talks about the title and the “sense of possibility that Christmas brings”: “some people see it [the winter] as an ending, with a whole story before it, but I see it very much as a new beginning.”

The song "Trains and Winter Rains", which premiered in UK radios on September 29, will be released as the album first single in November 10th too. "This track is my personal choice for the first single also" Enya says "I particularly enjoyed our percussion arrangement in this song." It's also described by Roma as a "journey from home that everyone must make when they start to get too comfortable." It features "Agnus Dei", a Christian verse commonly heard in churches.

“White is in the Winter Night”, described by Nicky Ryan as “the kind of song you could hear at the Proms with everyone joining in, it has that kind of Christmassy vibe”, is the album second single, released for dowload only in November 4th.

“O Come, O Come, Emmanuel” is a version of the Latin carol “Veni Veni”, rumored to be sung in Latin. “Oíche Chíun (Chorale)” was re-recorded after 20 years: Enya says that “It was exciting to re-live ‘Silent Night’ (...) It is re-released every year in America and it was so nice to go back and do something different with it.” The new rendering incorporates what Nicky describes as the “choir of one”, multi-layering Enya vocals. “I enjoy the process, I love the excitement of not knowing if it will work,” admits Enya.

"Midnight Blue", instrumental b-side from "Wild Child" single, has been reworked as "Stars and Midnight Blue", with added vocals. The song "And Winter Came" is an instrumental meant for reflection.

The last recorded song for the album, “My! My! Time Flies!”, is a tribute to the late Irish guitarist Jimmy Faulkner. The song involves a slight departure for Enya featuring guitar solos as well as references to The Beatles, Elvis and B.B King. Dubliner Pat Farrell is the featured guitarist on the track, which insiders are forecasting will be the third single, with a likely release date in the new year.

The bonus track "Miraculum" is available as an Amazon.com mp3 download.
